All About Cats

Cats are one of the most popular pets in the world — mysterious, independent, and endlessly fascinating. Here are a few things that make them so special:

Purring heals. A cat's purr vibrates at frequencies between 25–150 Hz, which research suggests may promote bone density and accelerate healing in soft tissue.
Cats sleep a lot. The average cat sleeps 12–16 hours a day, making them one of the sleepiest mammals on Earth.
They can't taste sweetness. Unlike most mammals, cats lack the taste receptor for sugar — they're obligate carnivores through and through.
Every nose is unique. A cat's nose print is as unique as a human fingerprint — no two are alike.
Cats communicate with humans differently than with other cats. Adult cats rarely meow at each other; the meow evolved almost entirely to communicate with people.

Whether they're knocking things off shelves or curling up in your lap, cats have been companions to humans for at least 10,000 years — and they show no signs of leaving.
